Fitstar by Fitbit

Fitbit Coach is a customized workout application for iPhone, Android, web, and wearables. Each time you're ready to workout, the algorithm creates a new personalized video workout you can do anywhere. The application works with data from Fitbit wearable devices to recommend fitness routines.

I was a product designer at Fitbit for 3 years, working on UX, visual, and interaction design problems.

iOS and Android

The core experience of the app is the workout experience, which leads users into their daily workout, and lets them select whether each move is "too easy" "just right", or "too hard". When they come back for their next session, there is a new session waiting for them, based on prior feedback.

Key features and product innovations in the app include smart workout recommendations based on fitness activity, fitness levelling, audio coaching, and integration with other Fitbit products.

FitStar Yoga

Fitstar Yoga was set up as a secondary experience to the original Fitstar (now Fitbit Coach) app. Created with the same overall architecture, the in-session experience had to be very different. Yoga is the practice of breath to movement, meaning the constant breaks for feedback that we implemented in Personal Trainer wouldn't work. Transitions lead in and out of each pose, with voice over instruction narrating the sequence. During the session, users can tap the "struggle button" always on screen, or pause to give full feedback. After the final resting pose, users are prompted to say whether key poses were "too easy, "just right", or "too hard".
