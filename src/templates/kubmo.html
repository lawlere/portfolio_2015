Glassbreakers

Mentorship for the modern woman.

Glassbreakers is a community for women to build their professional network, and meet peer mentors with similar career goals. The company started as a consumer product, and now is expanding into enterprise.

The team at Glassbreakers is lead by Lauren Mosenthal, a developer and product leader who I met at BDW. Along with the growing team of full time designers and engineers, there is a small team of design and copywriting contractors who work each week called "the forge". This contractor team meets on a weekly basis as needed. We work through problems with the product - white boarding, building, and designing.

TechWomen

TechWomen empowers, connects and supports the next generation of women leaders in science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM) from Africa, Central and South Asia, and the Middle East by providing them the access and opportunity needed to advance their careers, pursue their dreams, and inspire women and girls in their communities.

In September and October of 2016, I was an Impact Advisor for the team coming from Kenya. They ended up winning the pitch event, and bringing funding back to Kenya to implement their impact project, called One Desk, One Child.

In spring of 2017, I was selected to go on a delegation trip to Kenya to see what the TechWomen alumni were working on. It was amazing to see how women in tech work in Nairobi, you can read my blog post about it.
